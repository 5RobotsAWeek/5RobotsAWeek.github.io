Memristors are a sort of computing device for which the resistance of the component changes based on the current and voltage passed through the device. Once set, the resistance does not change until it is again connected to a voltage source, even if it is left for many years. The “freezing” characteristic of the memristors make them ideal for nonlinear analog computing circuits. This video compares the motions of a self-balancing robot that uses a traditional digital processor and one that uses a hybrid of digital cirtcuit elements and memristors.
