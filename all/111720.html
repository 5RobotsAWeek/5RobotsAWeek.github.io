Logic gates are fundemental to any computing system. Today, these gates are made with small 0silicon transistors. Before transistors, however, lightbulb-size vacuum triodes formed the base of room-filling calculators, like those used by NASA during the 1960's space race. This article discusses how triodes work, and why they still have a few (limited) applications today. (Image is from Wikipedia article "Triode".)
