Humans can recognize tons of objects on sight, instantly noting relevant similarities and differences between one object and similar items. This capability has historically been difficult to translate to an automated system because there is so much variability to account for. But with the introduction of neural networks, programs are able to use sets of labeled images to “learn” to classify camera input. This has many applications. For example, in industry this deep learning can help speed up the process of quality control and product identification (this application is further discussed in the article and video).
For a more in-depth discussion of the concepts behind neural networks, 3blue1brown’s youtube series is a wonderful resource. Link to Part 1
