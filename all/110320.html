Humans have long been fascinated by self-driven mechanical devices. Before the concept of a robot was introduced in the 1920s, automatons were the object of this curiosity. One such machine is the floating orchestra, a musical boat driven by the force of water. It was built by Arabic mathematician and inventor Ismail al-Jazari, who explained the creation in his Book of Knowledge of Ingenious Mechanical Devices in 1206. Because it could be adjusted to play different songs, some credit the floating orchestra as the first programmable robot.
