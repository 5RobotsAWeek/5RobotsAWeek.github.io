Monday 11/30/2020
This robotic gripper manipulates cables. The system, designed by researchers in MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intellegence Laboratory (CSAIL), uses high-resolution tactile sensors mounted on the soft-robotic fingertips to determine the size, shape, and pliability of the chord. Though the robotic system is not yet perfected -- among other factors, the researchers plan to experiment with finger shape -- this gripper has potential to lead to robots that help with mundane tasks which require manipulation of soft objects (like knot tying and cloth folding).
