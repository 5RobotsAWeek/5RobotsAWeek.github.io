Proccessing systems are digital, which means they use discrete values-- ones and zeros -- to analyze information. However, most sensory input is analog, meaning that there is a continuous range of possible input values. In order to convert this analog input to digital, a system will sample the input values, and interpret the original signal from those values. Fundemental to making such a system work properly is sampling often enough to get a reasonable idea of the original form. This video and article discuss the Nyquist-Shannon Theorem, which gives the minimum sampling frequecy based on the signal's input frequency.
